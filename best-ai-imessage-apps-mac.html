Four good choices, four different jobs

Start with the action you want to remove. If you still want to review every message, a drafting tool is the safer and simpler choice. If the problem is switching among Slack, WhatsApp, email, and iMessage, use a unified inbox. If you need the Mac to answer a routine one-to-one text while you are busy, look for explicit automatic sending and clear controls.

Best built-in option

1. Apple Smart Reply

Apple Smart Reply is the best starting point for people who want suggestions without installing another app. In Messages, you select a conversation, click the text field, and choose a suggested response. Apple places the draft in the text field. You can refine it with Writing Tools and press Return when it is ready.

The human send step is the strength and the limit. Nothing leaves without your review, but it does not solve hands-free answering. Apple says Apple Intelligence requires an Apple silicon Mac, macOS Sequoia 15.1 or later, 7 GB of device storage, and supported matching device and Siri languages. Availability varies by feature, region, and language.

Apple has also announced that Smart Reply will draw on a user's personalized writing style in macOS 27, planned for autumn 2026. Sources: Apple's current Messages Smart Reply guide, Apple Intelligence requirements, and June 2026 announcement.

Best focused auto-send value

2. GhostReply

GhostReply is for one narrow outcome: answer one-to-one iMessages like you would while a Mac app runs. It reads recent conversation history, builds a local style profile, generates a reply through Groq, and sends through Messages. You can target one person or everyone, set a reply mode and speed, and take over manually. Group chats are skipped.

The purchase is 10 free replies and then $4.99 once. You bring a Groq API key, grant Full Disk Access and Messages Automation permissions, keep Messages signed in, and leave GhostReply running. History and profiles stay local. Relevant message context goes directly from your Mac to Groq for inference and does not pass through GhostReply's servers.

The main limit is automatic sending itself. Keyword triage, group skipping, manual takeover, and AI-output cleanup reduce obvious mistakes but cannot guarantee a good reply. Use it for routine, low-risk conversations, not emergencies or consequential decisions. See GhostReply vs Apple Intelligence for the assisted-versus-automatic tradeoff.

Best broader conversation toolkit

3. Gabby

Gabby goes well beyond reply generation. Its official page lists tone analysis, smart replies, message search, scheduled messaging, conversation insights, contact CRM, and fixed or AI auto-reply. It reads iMessage, SMS, and RCS from Apple Messages. The paid auto-reply can run per conversation or globally.

The Free plan currently lists 15 on-device AI analyses and 5 smart replies per month. Plus lists $4.99 per month billed yearly, with your API key and auto-reply. Pro lists $11.99 per month billed yearly with included AI. Gabby's FAQ also lists month-to-month prices of $6.99 and $14.99. It requires macOS 13 or later, and its iPhone product is still described as coming soon.

Choose Gabby when analysis, search, scheduling, and CRM justify a subscription. Choose GhostReply when you only want a lightweight automatic responder and one-time pricing. Source: Gabby's official feature and pricing page. The deeper breakdown is on GhostReply vs Gabby.

Best multi-platform inbox

4. NOX, formerly found as RPLY

NOX is the choice for people whose problem is fragmented communication. Its site advertises ten platforms, including iMessage, WhatsApp, Slack, Telegram, Signal, Instagram, X, LinkedIn, Discord, and email. It adds cross-platform search, urgency triage, conversation memory, and AI drafts in one Mac window.

NOX explicitly says it never sends automatically. You edit, approve, or ignore each suggestion. It requires an Apple silicon Mac with macOS 14 Sonoma or later. The current page shows $25 per month billed annually, while its FAQ says Pro is $30 per month. Because those figures conflict, the current month-to-month price is Unknown until checkout confirms it.

Choose NOX when ten-platform consolidation is worth more than automatic sending. Choose a focused iMessage tool when your job begins and ends in Messages. Source: NOX's official product and pricing page. See the direct RPLY alternative comparison.

How to choose safely

  1. Choose draft-only if every message needs your approval.
  2. Choose auto-send only for low-risk contacts and routine topics.
  3. Check whether inference is on-device, direct to a model provider, or vendor-hosted.
  4. Confirm recurring pricing at checkout when a public page is inconsistent.
  5. Test manual takeover, group behavior, and app-off behavior before leaving it unattended.

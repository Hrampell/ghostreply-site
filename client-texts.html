AI iMessage Auto-Reply for Client Texts on Mac

Acknowledge routine updates and scheduling questions while you work, then take over before the conversation reaches money, contracts, confidential details, or professional advice.

For one professional on one Mac, not a client service platform.

Keep small client questions from breaking focus

Freelancers, consultants, contractors, and local service providers often use iMessage as a lightweight client channel. A client may ask whether a file arrived or whether an appointment can move while you are doing the work they hired you to do.

GhostReply watches eligible incoming one-to-one chats while it runs on your Mac. It uses local conversation history and relevant context to generate a short reply through Groq, then sends the message automatically. That can cover simple acknowledgments, but it should not replace judgment where a wrong reply could change the scope, price, deadline, confidentiality, or outcome of the work.

Three routine client text examples

These examples show the low-stakes lane. Output varies with the thread and can be wrong, so avoid language that confirms a fact or commitment you have not checked.

Incoming: Did the new files come through?

Possible reply: got them thank you, ill review everything and follow up

Incoming: Could we move our check-in to tomorrow afternoon?

Possible reply: im checking tomorrow now and will confirm a time shortly

Incoming: Any update on the first draft?

Possible reply: im working through it now and will send you a proper update today

Give one client a narrow reply window

Start with One person mode for a client whose routine communication you understand. Pick a style and reply delay, then keep the Terminal session visible during the free trial. If you send a manual reply after GhostReply has answered, the one-person session stops.

All-contacts mode can respond to incoming one-to-one chats across personal and work contacts. In that mode, your manual message pauses that contact for 30 minutes while other eligible conversations continue. Group chats are skipped. Requirements and setup for client messages

  1. Use an awake, internet-connected Mac with Messages signed in to the iMessage account you want to monitor.
  2. Install GhostReply in Terminal and grant that terminal app Full Disk Access to read local Messages history.
  3. Grant Messages Automation permission so the terminal can send. Contacts access helps identify saved clients.
  4. Add your own Groq API key, select one person or all eligible one-to-one chats, and choose the reply style and delay.

GhostReply keeps history access, the learned profile, configuration, and statistics local on the Mac. Selected conversation samples and relevant context go directly from your Mac to Groq for profile and reply generation. Message content does not pass through GhostReply servers. License validation, trial accounting, and analytics data can reach GhostReply services.

The app can only send while the Mac is awake, online, and running GhostReply. Close it to stop. Use the Mac iMessage auto-reply setup guide for the complete permission and trial steps.

Keep consequential client work manual

Do not let GhostReply accept a contract, change scope, approve a price, promise a deadline, provide financial, legal, or medical guidance, share payment instructions, expose confidential information, or handle a regulated or emergency matter. Avoid automatic sending whenever the reply could bind you, reveal protected data, or cause the client to act.

Urgent and sensitive filters are heuristic keyword checks. They can miss unusual language and are not a confidentiality, compliance, or professional review system. Follow your client agreements and industry rules, use a proper recordkeeping channel when required, and read the AI iMessage auto-reply safety guide before testing.
